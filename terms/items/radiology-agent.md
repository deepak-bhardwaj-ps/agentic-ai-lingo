Radiology agent is a loose term for an AI agent used in radiology work.

In practice, it usually means software that helps with parts of a radiology workflow, such as sorting studies, routing work, pulling in patient context, drafting support text, or helping manage follow-up tasks. It does not mean the software replaces the radiologist.

The term matters because radiology has many repeatable tasks and a lot of information to juggle. A radiology agent can help reduce delays and admin work by making small decisions or taking next steps inside a workflow.

It is not a formal medical category, and people do not all use it the same way. It is not the same as a diagnostic model, a chatbot, or a full autonomous doctor. In most real systems, a human still reviews important clinical decisions.
