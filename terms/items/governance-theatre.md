Governance theatre is when a group has rules, meetings, checks, or approval steps that make it look like something is being controlled, but in practice very little changes.

In real life, this can mean people hold review meetings, write policies, or fill in forms, yet the same risky choices still get made the same way. The process is visible, but it does not strongly guide behaviour.

The term matters because real governance should help people make safer and better decisions. If governance is only for show, problems can keep happening even though the organisation looks responsible on paper.

It is not the same as proper oversight. Good governance has clear owners, real decisions, and follow-through. Governance theatre is mostly appearance without enough action.
