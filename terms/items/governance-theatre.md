Governance theatre is when an organisation has rules, meetings, or approval steps that look like control, but do not really change what happens.

In practice, it means people can point to policies, forms, or review panels and say “we have governance”, while risky decisions still go through in the same way.

The term matters because real governance should shape decisions, assign responsibility, and create follow-through. If it is only for show, the organisation may look careful without being safer.

It is not the same as proper oversight. It is also not just having a policy document. Real governance changes behaviour; governance theatre mostly changes appearances.
