A clinical documentation agent is an AI tool that helps turn a patient visit into a draft clinical note.

In practice, it listens to or reads parts of the visit, pulls out the useful details, and writes a note that a clinician can review and edit before it goes into the medical record.

The term matters because clinicians spend a lot of time on paperwork. A tool like this can reduce typing and help records be written faster, but it still needs human review.

It is not a doctor, and it is not the same as a general chatbot. It is also not just a voice recorder or simple transcription tool, because its job is to help produce a usable clinical note.
