Quantitative research agent is a tool that helps people study data to find patterns, test ideas, and make research reports in finance.

In practice, it usually looks at numbers, market data, company filings, news, and other facts, then helps a person compare ideas or build a case for a trading strategy. It may search sources, organise information, run simple analysis, and draft findings.

The term matters because quantitative finance uses a lot of data, and an agent can speed up the slow parts of research. But the output still needs human checking, because markets change and bad data can lead to bad decisions.

It is not a magic stock picker. It is also not the same as a normal chatbot, because the point is to do structured research work, not just answer questions in plain text.
