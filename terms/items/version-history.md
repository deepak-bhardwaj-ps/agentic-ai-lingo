Version history is the list of earlier saved versions of a page or file.

In a wiki or shared document, each change can create a new version. Version history lets you see what changed, who changed it, and sometimes restore an older version.

It matters because people can make mistakes, delete useful text, or disagree about what changed. Version history gives you a way to check the past and go back if needed.

It is not the same as software version numbers like 1.2.3. In this glossary, it means saved past copies or edits of content, not product releases.
