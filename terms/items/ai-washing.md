AI washing is when a product is described as using AI, but the claim makes the product sound more advanced than it really is.

In practice, it means the label is bigger than the reality. A company might say something is AI-driven when it mostly follows fixed rules, uses ordinary software, or has only a small AI part.

This matters because people may trust the product too much, pay more for it, or expect it to do things it cannot do.

It is not the same as a product using no AI at all. It is about misleading claims, not just the presence of AI.
