Control washing is when something is talked about as if it has strong controls, but the real controls are weak, missing, or hard to verify.

In practice, it means looking past the promise and checking the facts. Are there real [[Guardrails|guardrails]]? Can people override the system? Is there logging, human review, and a named owner who is accountable? If not, the claim about control may be misleading.

The term matters because people can trust a system too much if the controls are only on paper. That can lead to bad decisions, unsafe behaviour, or problems nobody is clearly responsible for.

Control washing is not a formal technical test. It is a criticism of how something is described. It does not prove the system is unsafe by itself. It says the claim of strong control needs evidence.
