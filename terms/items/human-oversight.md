Human oversight means a person can watch what an AI system is doing and step in if it starts to make a bad choice.

In practice, this can mean a human checks the AI’s output before it is sent, approves a risky action, or can stop the system if something looks wrong. The person needs enough information and real authority to intervene, not just a title on a process chart.

This matters because AI systems can make mistakes, miss context, or produce confident but wrong answers. Human oversight is one way to reduce harm when the result affects money, safety, rights, or important decisions.

It is not the same as “we have a human somewhere in the process”. If the person cannot see enough detail, cannot act in time, or is expected to approve everything automatically, then the oversight is weak or fake.
