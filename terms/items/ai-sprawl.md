AI sprawl is the uncontrolled growth of AI tools, models, automations, or agents across an organisation without one clear way to track and manage them.

In practice, different teams may buy or build their own AI systems, connect them to different data, and set their own rules. That can create duplicate tools, higher costs, uneven security, and confusion about which systems are approved, useful, or still needed.

The term matters because AI systems can spread quickly. If no one keeps track, an organisation can lose visibility, miss security problems, and struggle to remove tools that are no longer needed.

It is not a formal technical standard. It is a shorthand label for a management problem. It is also not the same as simply using many AI tools. An organisation can use lots of AI safely if each system has clear ownership, approval, and review.
