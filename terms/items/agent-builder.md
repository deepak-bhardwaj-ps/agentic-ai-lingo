An agent builder is a person who creates AI agents.

In practice, an agent builder chooses what the agent should do, wires up its tools and data, and checks that it behaves sensibly. They may work in a visual builder, with prompts, or in code.

The term matters because building an agent is more than writing one prompt. Someone has to decide the steps, connect the tools, and test the result.

This is not yet a strict, standard job title. People sometimes use it to mean a product feature, a no-code builder, or a developer who builds agents. Those uses overlap, so the meaning depends on context.
