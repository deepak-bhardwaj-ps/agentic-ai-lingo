AI bot swarms is a loose term for many automated or semi-automated accounts acting together online.

In practice, it usually means a group of accounts posting, liking, replying, or sharing in a coordinated way. The accounts may be fully automated, partly automated, or controlled by people using software to work faster and at larger scale.

The point is not just that the accounts exist. The point is that they act together in a way that can make a message look more popular, drown out other voices, or push a false impression of public support.

This is not a strict technical term. It does not prove the accounts are bots, and it does not prove bad intent on its own. It is also not the same as normal automation, such as scheduled updates, reminders, or customer support replies.

In short, the term is useful when there is evidence of coordination plus a real effect on what people see online.
