Research engineer is a job role for someone who uses coding to help turn research ideas into working systems.

In practice, a research engineer usually sits between a researcher and a software engineer. They build experiments, test ideas, improve models, and make sure the code is strong enough to run at scale.

This role matters because many modern AI systems only improve when theory, experiments, and engineering work happen together.

It is not exactly the same as a research scientist. A research scientist usually focuses more on new ideas and scientific questions, while a research engineer focuses more on building, testing, and scaling those ideas with code. Different companies may use the title slightly differently.
