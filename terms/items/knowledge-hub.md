Knowledge hub is a central place where people can find organised information, documents, and links.

In practice, it is a page, workspace, or site that brings useful knowledge together so people do not have to search across lots of tools. It may include guides, FAQs, policies, project notes, or links to the systems that hold the real source material.

The term matters because a good knowledge hub saves time and helps people find the right answer without asking the same question again and again. It also makes important information easier to keep up to date.

It is not just a homepage, dashboard, or chatbot. A real knowledge hub organises knowledge and makes it easier to use. It is also not exactly the same as a knowledge base: a knowledge hub is often the broader place people go to reach that knowledge.
