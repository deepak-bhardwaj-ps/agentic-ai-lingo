Agent washing is when ordinary automation, a chatbot, or a simple workflow is marketed as if it were a real agent.

In practice, it means the product sounds more independent than it is. The marketing may suggest the system can plan, choose tools, and keep going on its own, but in reality it may only follow fixed steps or need frequent human help.

This matters because people can end up trusting the product too much. They may buy the wrong tool, expect it to do jobs it cannot do, or miss the checks needed to use it safely.

It is not a formal technical category. It is a warning term for claims that are stronger than the system behind them.
