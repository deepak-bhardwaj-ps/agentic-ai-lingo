An agentic engineering manager is an engineering manager who leads teams building AI systems that can plan steps, use tools, and carry out work.

In practice, this role is about helping a team ship those systems safely and reliably. That means setting priorities, checking quality, coordinating work, and making sure the agent does not create more problems than it solves.

The term matters because agentic systems can take actions, not just answer questions. That makes management work more than schedule tracking. Someone has to think about reliability, failure modes, human review, and whether the system is actually useful in production.

This is not a standard universal job title. Different companies may use it to mean slightly different things. It is also not the same as a general engineering manager role, and it is not just prompt writing.
