Agentic propaganda is a loose label for propaganda spread by AI agents or other automated systems that do the work with little human help.

In practice, it means posts, comments, images, or messages are being created or repeated by software to shape what people think. The key idea is not just that a machine was used. The system is trying to persuade, mislead, or make one side look more popular by producing large amounts of similar content, often across many accounts.

This term matters because automated influence campaigns can spread faster than people can check them. That can make false stories look popular, hide who is behind a message, and make it harder to tell what is real online.

It is not a precise technical category. It is not the same as normal automation, such as a customer service bot or a scheduled post. It is also not proven just because AI helped write something. To use the term well, there should be evidence of coordination, deception, or manipulation.
