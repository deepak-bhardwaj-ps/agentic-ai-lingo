Tax agent usually means an AI agent built to help with tax work.

In practice, it may read tax documents, check rules, collect facts, draft answers, or help prepare and review tax returns. It is a specialist helper for tax tasks, not a general chat bot.

This term matters because tax work uses messy rules, changing laws, and lots of paperwork. A tax agent can save time, but the output still needs careful human checking.

It is not the same as a human registered tax agent. In Australia, that phrase means a qualified and registered tax professional. It is also not a fixed legal or technical standard for AI. People often use it loosely to mean a tax-focused AI tool or assistant.
