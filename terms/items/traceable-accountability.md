Traceable accountability means being able to show who is responsible for a decision or action, what they were allowed to do, and what actually happened.

In practice, it means the system needs clear owners, clear rules, and a record that can be checked later. That record might include logs, approvals, changes, and handovers. If something goes wrong, people should be able to trace it back to the right person, team, or step.

This matters because AI systems can affect people in ways that are hard to see. If nobody can trace responsibility, mistakes are harder to fix and harms are harder to explain.

It is not just a log file. A log only records events. Traceable accountability also needs named responsibility, limits on authority, and a way to review or challenge decisions.
