An agent collective is a group of software agents that coordinate with one another.

In practice, each agent does its own job, but the agents affect each other's work. They may pass messages, split up tasks, check one another's answers, or change plan when new information appears.

The term matters because one agent is often enough for a small task, but several agents can handle larger jobs more safely or efficiently. For example, one agent can gather facts, another can review them, and a third can turn them into a final response.

It is not a formal standard. It does not automatically mean the agents share memory, have a leader, or follow one agreed rulebook. It is also not just a list of agents running one after another without real coordination.
