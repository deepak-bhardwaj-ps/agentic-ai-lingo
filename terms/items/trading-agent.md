Trading agent is an AI system used for trading work.

In practice, it looks at things like prices, charts, news, company reports, and risk signals, then helps a person decide whether to buy, sell, or hold. Some trading agents only suggest actions. Others can place trades, but they still need human checks.

The term matters because trading is fast, data-heavy, and full of small decisions. An agent can help sort information and react more quickly than a person working alone.

It is not the same as a general chatbot that talks about money. It is also not just a normal trading algorithm, because the term usually implies a system that can read information, reason about it, and take or recommend actions.

The term is still used in a loose way, so different teams may mean slightly different things by it.
