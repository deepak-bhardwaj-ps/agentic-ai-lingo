A forward deployed engineer is a software engineer who works closely with a customer to build, adapt, and deploy software for that customer’s real needs.

In practice, the engineer spends time with the customer, learns their systems, writes code, tests changes, and helps roll the software out. They also pass what they learn back to the company’s main product and engineering teams.

The term matters because it is used for work that sits between normal product engineering and customer delivery. In AI companies, it is often used for the people who turn model demos into working systems for a specific customer.

It is not just a sales role. It is also not the same as a standard software engineer who only builds one product for many users.

The term is still not used in exactly the same way everywhere. Some companies mean a highly customer-facing engineer; others use it for a role that is closer to solutions engineering or professional services.
