Knowledge compounding is the idea that a knowledge system becomes more useful each time you add, connect, and clean up information.

In practice, this usually means a wiki or note system where new pages do not sit alone. They link to older pages, reuse existing ideas, and fix earlier mistakes. Over time, the whole system becomes easier to search, easier to trust, and easier to build on.

The term matters because good knowledge systems do not just store facts. They help a person or team avoid repeating work, spot contradictions, and improve earlier notes instead of starting from zero every time.

It is not a formal scientific law. It is also not the same as simply reading more, storing more files, or making an AI answer look better. The compounding comes from linking, updating, and reusing knowledge over time.
