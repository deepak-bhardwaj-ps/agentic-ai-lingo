A context graph is a connected map of facts, relationships, and past actions that helps an AI system work out what matters right now.

In practice, it links people, things, events, rules, and decisions so the system can pull the most useful context for the current task instead of guessing from a pile of separate notes.

This matters because AI systems do not understand everything at once. A context graph helps them find the right background, follow links between related items, and use that information in a more sensible way.

It is not just a graph database, and it is not the same as a simple list of documents or chat logs. It also does not automatically make the AI truthful or correct. The graph still depends on good data, clear rules, and careful updates.
