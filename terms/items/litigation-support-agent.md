Litigation support agent is not a strict legal standard term. It usually means an AI tool or assistant that helps legal teams with litigation work.

In practice, it may help sort documents, find useful evidence, build timelines, summarise case files, or support e-discovery work. It is there to speed up repetitive parts of litigation, not to decide the case.

The term matters because litigation creates a lot of documents and evidence. A tool that can help organise that material can save time and reduce manual work.

It is not just a general legal chatbot. It is also not the same as a lawyer, and it should not be treated as making legal decisions on its own. The term is still a bit unsettled, so different companies may use it in slightly different ways.
