An agentic risk analyst is a person who looks for risks in AI systems that can take actions on their own, not just answer questions.

In practice, this person watches for things like unsafe tool use, bad decisions, data leaks, weak approvals, and new ways people might misuse the system. They turn those signals into clear risk calls so teams know what needs fixing, what needs escalation, and what can wait.

The term matters because agentic systems can do more than chat. They may search, click, send, write, or trigger actions. That means small mistakes can turn into real-world harm faster than in a normal chatbot.

It is not a formal universal job title, and it is not just a cyber role or a finance risk role. It is best understood as a cross between product risk, safety, security, and governance work focused on AI agents.
