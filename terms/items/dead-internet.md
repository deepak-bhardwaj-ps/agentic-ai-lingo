Dead Internet is a slang term for the belief that the internet is becoming dominated by bots, spam, and machine-made content instead of real human activity.

In practice, people use it when online spaces feel repetitive, fake, or crowded with automated posts. It is usually a claim, fear, or complaint, not a measured fact.

The term matters because bot traffic, spam, fake accounts, and synthetic content are real problems. They can make search results worse, spread junk faster, and make it harder to trust what you see online.

It is not a formal technical term, and it is not proof that most of the internet is “dead”. When people use it loosely, they may be talking about real problems, but the stronger claim is not settled and should not be treated as fact.
