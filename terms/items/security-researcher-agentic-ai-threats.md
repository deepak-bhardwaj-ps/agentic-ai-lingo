A security researcher, agentic AI threats is someone who studies how AI agents can be attacked, misused, or pushed into unsafe actions.

In practice, this person looks for weak spots in systems that can plan, use tools, access data, and take steps on their own. They may test how an agent can be tricked, what damage it could do with too much access, and which safeguards stop that from happening.

The term matters because AI agents can do more than answer questions. They can search, click, write, send, and trigger actions, so a small security flaw can turn into a real-world problem faster than with a normal chatbot.

This is not a general security researcher role with no agent focus, and it is not just offensive hacking. It is a still-emerging label for threat research focused on AI agents and the controls that keep them safe.
