A legal agent is a software tool that helps with legal work by reading documents, finding key clauses, and suggesting changes.

In practice, it is used for jobs like contract review, redlining, comparing versions, and checking whether a document follows a set playbook. Some products put it inside Word or another document editor so a legal team can work in the same place where they write and review files.

The term matters because legal work is full of repetitive reading and checking. A legal agent can save time and help people catch issues faster. But the human still needs to make the final judgement, because legal mistakes can have serious consequences.

It is not a human legal agent. It is not a lawyer. It is also not a settled technical standard, because different companies use the term in slightly different ways.
