A bot swarm is a group of automated accounts or software agents that act together online, usually to push a message, flood a conversation, or make something seem more popular than it really is.

In practice, the term is used when many bots behave in a similar way at about the same time. They may post, like, reply, or share in a coordinated pattern. The point is not just that the accounts are automated. The point is that they work together.

This matters because a swarm can distort what people think is happening. It can make a small idea look widely supported, bury other voices, or create noise so normal conversation becomes hard to follow.

It is not a precise technical term. It is also not the same as one bot, one spam account, or every large group of accounts. Some automated activity is harmless, such as sending reminders or updates. The term is usually used when the behaviour is coordinated and meant to influence people or disrupt discussion.
