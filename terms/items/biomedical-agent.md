Biomedical agent is not a single strict standard. It usually means an AI agent used for biomedical work, such as searching papers, checking claims, analysing health data, or helping with research tasks.

In practice, the agent may plan steps, use tools, read domain sources, and produce answers or actions that help researchers or clinicians. It is not just a chatbot that replies from memory.

The term matters because biomedical information is complex and easy to get wrong. A biomedical agent is meant to help people work with that information more quickly and more systematically.

It is not the same as a medical device, a doctor, or proof that a system is safe in real clinical use. It is also not a fully settled category, so different teams may use the term in slightly different ways.
