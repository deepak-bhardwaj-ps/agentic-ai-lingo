A quantitative trader is a person who makes trading decisions using data, maths, and computer models.

In practice, that means they look for patterns in markets, test ideas with historical data, and use code or trading systems to help decide when to buy or sell. In many firms, they also watch how the strategy behaves in real time and adjust it when market conditions change.

The term matters because it describes a real job in finance where coding, statistics, and trading are all part of the work. It is common in firms such as hedge funds, market makers, and other trading companies.

It is not the same as a normal trader who mainly relies on instinct or news. It is also not the same as a quantitative researcher, who is more focused on building and testing the models than on trading them.
