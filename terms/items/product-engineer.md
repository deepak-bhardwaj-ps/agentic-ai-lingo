A product engineer is a person who helps decide what should be built and also builds it.

In practice, that means they talk to users, look at customer data, choose sensible features to work on, and then write the code to make them real. They care about whether the product actually helps people, not just whether the code works.

The term matters because some teams want engineers who can work closer to customers and move faster with less hand-off between roles. It is often used in small product teams and newer software companies.

It is not the same as a product manager. It is also not just a normal software engineer with a fancier name. The key difference is that a product engineer helps shape the problem and the solution, not only the technical implementation.
