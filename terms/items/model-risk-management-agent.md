A model risk management agent is an AI helper that supports the work of model risk management.

In banking, model risk management means checking that important models are built well, used properly, monitored, and kept under control. An agent for this job might help sort model inventories, draft documentation, track validation tasks, or flag unusual behaviour for a human to review.

The term matters because banks use many models, and mistakes in them can cause financial loss or bad decisions. An agent can make the work faster, but it still needs human oversight.

This is not the same as a model itself. It is also not a formal regulator-approved term. Current US banking guidance treats generative AI and agentic AI as outside the scope of model risk management guidance, so the phrase is best understood as an emerging helper term, not a settled category.
