A contract review agent is an AI tool that helps people read contracts and spot what matters, such as important clauses, missing terms, unusual wording, and possible risks.

In practice, it may compare a contract against a checklist or playbook, highlight sections that do not match, suggest redlines, and pull out key details like deadlines or responsibilities. It is usually used by legal teams or business teams that need a first pass before a person makes the final decision.

This matters because contracts are long, repetitive, and easy to miss important details in. A contract review agent can save time and make reviews more consistent.

It is not a lawyer, and it is not the final authority. It can help with review, but a human must check the result, especially for legal judgement, local law, and high-stakes decisions.
