Decision sovereignty means deciding who has the final say when an AI system suggests or carries out an action.

In practice, it means naming the person or role that can approve, stop, or reverse the action, and making sure that choice is recorded.

This matters because AI can act quickly, but it should not quietly take over decisions that people or teams are meant to own. If the wrong person or system has the final say, mistakes can spread fast and be hard to trace.

It is not the same as writing a rule in a policy document. Real decision sovereignty needs a named owner, clear limits, a way to stop or reverse the action, and proof such as logs or approvals.

It is also not a claim that the AI is in charge. The point is the opposite: to keep final authority with the human role or organisation that is supposed to hold it.
