A drug discovery agent is a software agent that helps with parts of finding new medicines.

In practice, it may search research papers, suggest molecules, rank candidates, check likely side effects, or help plan experiments. It does not do all of drug discovery by itself. People still have to set the goal, review the results, and test the ideas in real life.

The term matters because drug discovery has many steps and lots of data. An agent can help handle some of the repeated work and move between steps more quickly.

It is not the same as a general chat tool. It is not just a prediction model. It is also not a finished medicine-making system. The term is still emerging, so different teams may use it in slightly different ways.
