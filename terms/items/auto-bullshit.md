Auto-bullshit is informal slang for AI text that sounds confident and polished but is wrong, unsupported, or made up.

In practice, people use it when a model gives an answer that looks polished but falls apart when you check it. The words may sound certain, but the facts are weak, missing, or invented.

The term matters because fluent AI can be convincing even when it is wrong. That can mislead people into trusting bad advice, bad summaries, or made-up explanations.

It is not a formal technical term. In careful writing, it is better to say exactly what went wrong, such as hallucination, unsupported claim, or false output. Some people use it as a blunt way of saying the model is producing text without regard for truth, but that is a judgement, not a standard technical definition.
