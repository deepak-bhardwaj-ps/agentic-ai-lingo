Clinical copilot is a not-quite-fixed term for an AI assistant that helps healthcare workers with clinical tasks, especially documentation and routine workflow steps.

In practice, it usually helps draft notes, capture patient-clinician conversations, surface information, or speed up forms and other admin work inside a healthcare system.

The term matters because clinicians spend a lot of time on paperwork. A clinical copilot can save time and reduce repetitive work, but it should support the clinician rather than replace clinical judgement.

It is not a doctor, and it is not a guarantee of safe medical advice. If a tool only answers health questions without helping with clinical work, it is usually better described as a healthcare chatbot or assistant instead.
