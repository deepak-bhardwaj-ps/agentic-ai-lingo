An applied scientist is a person who uses science, maths, and machine learning to solve practical problems.

In practice, an applied scientist usually works on real business or product problems. They may build models, run experiments, analyse data, and help turn research ideas into something people can use.

The term matters because it shows the work is not just theory. It is about using technical knowledge to make a system work better, faster, safer, or more useful.

It is not exactly the same in every company. In many places, it overlaps with roles such as research scientist, machine learning engineer, or data scientist. The title usually means a mix of research and product work, not pure academic science.
