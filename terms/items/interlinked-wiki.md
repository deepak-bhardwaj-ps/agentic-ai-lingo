An interlinked wiki is a wiki whose pages point to one another with links.

In practice, that means you can jump from one page to a related page, then keep following links through the rest of the wiki. Some systems also show backlinks, which are links from other pages back to the page you are reading.

This matters because linked pages are easier to explore than a flat list of separate notes. The links help people find related ideas, follow a topic, and build a web of connected information.

This is not a strict technical standard. It is just a clear way to describe a wiki that is connected by links. It is also not the same thing as a knowledge graph, which usually stores relationships in a more structured way.
