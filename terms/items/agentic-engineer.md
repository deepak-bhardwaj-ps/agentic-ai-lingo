An agentic engineer is a person who builds and supervises AI systems that can plan steps, use tools, and carry out tasks.

In practice, that means designing how the system works, connecting it to tools and data, checking its outputs, adding safety rules, and watching how it behaves in real use.

The term matters because these systems can do more than chat. They can take actions, so the person building them has to think about reliability, limits, and what happens when the system makes a mistake.

This is not a fixed, universal job title. Different teams may use it to mean something close to agent developer or AI engineer. It is also not the same as prompt writing alone.
