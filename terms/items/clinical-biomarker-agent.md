Clinical biomarker agent is not a standard medical term. In this glossary, it means an AI agent that helps find, check, or explain biomarkers using clinical data.

In practice, that could mean looking through patient measurements, lab results, scans, or medical records to spot patterns that might matter for diagnosis, risk, or treatment response. It may also help compare those patterns with medical literature so a human can judge whether the signal is believable.

The term matters because biomarker work is often slow and data-heavy. An agent can help organise the evidence, rank candidates, and make the reasoning easier to follow.

It is not the same as a doctor, a lab test, or a proven biomarker. It also does not mean the system has discovered a useful biomarker just because it found a pattern. Any real clinical use still needs expert review and validation.
