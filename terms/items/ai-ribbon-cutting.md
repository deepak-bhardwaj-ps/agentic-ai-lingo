AI ribbon-cutting is slang for an AI launch that looks important but has not yet proved itself in everyday use.

In practice, it usually means there was a demo, a press release, a pilot, or a big announcement, but the tool is not yet helping people do their normal work every day.

The term matters because a flashy launch can hide weak results. An AI system is only useful if people actually use it, it works reliably, and it solves a real problem after launch.

It is not a formal technical term. It is also not the same as a finished system or a successful rollout. It points to the gap between a public celebration and real, everyday value.

If someone says a company is doing AI ribbon-cutting, they usually mean the company is celebrating the start of AI, not proving that AI is delivering value yet.
