A fraud analyst is a person whose job is to look for fraud, investigate suspicious activity, and help reduce losses.

In practice, a fraud analyst checks patterns in transactions, accounts, claims, or user behaviour. They may review alerts, gather evidence, write reports, and pass serious cases to investigators or other teams.

The term matters because fraud can cost people and organisations money, data, and trust. Fraud analysts help spot problems early, before more damage happens.

This is not a fraud detection tool or software system. It is a human role. It is also not always exactly the same as a fraud investigator, because some organisations use those job titles differently.
