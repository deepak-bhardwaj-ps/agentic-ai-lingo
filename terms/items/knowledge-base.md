A knowledge base is an organised collection of articles or pages that helps people find answers quickly.

In practice, it is a place where a company, team, or community stores useful information such as how-to guides, common questions, troubleshooting steps, and policies. People can search it instead of asking the same question again and again.

It matters because it saves time, reduces repeated support work, and keeps important knowledge in one place. A good knowledge base also helps new people learn faster.

It is not just a folder of random files, and it is not the same as a database of raw data. It is also not the same as a chatbot, although a chatbot may use a knowledge base to answer questions.
