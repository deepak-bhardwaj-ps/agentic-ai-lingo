Clinical decision support agent is not a fixed medical term. It usually means an AI agent that helps a clinician make a care decision by finding relevant information, checking patient details, and suggesting possible next steps.

In practice, it might read a patient record, pull in guidelines, flag risks, or draft options for review. A clinician is still supposed to judge the advice and make the final decision.

The term matters because hospitals and health software teams are starting to use agent-style systems for clinical support. That can help with speed and consistency, but it also raises safety, accuracy, and responsibility questions.

It is not the same as a general chatbot, and it is not the same as a fully autonomous doctor. It is also not always a formal regulatory label. In most cases, it is best understood as a specific kind of clinical decision support tool built with agent-like behaviour.
